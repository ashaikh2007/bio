Diversity



All life on Earth can be classified based on certain traits or its evolutionary past. But what defines life? Scientists have come up with many ways to determine if something is considered living or not


Chemical Composition

Of the millions of organisms on this Earth, all are made of the same 25 elements. The chemical composition of something can determine if it's living or non living. You can use the acronym 'SPONCH' to remember the key elements

Sulfur - Makes up trace amounts of living organisms

Phosphorus - Makes up 1% of living organisms

Oxygen - Makes up 65% of living organisms

Nitrogen - Makes up 3.3% of living organisms

Carbon - Makes up 18.5% of living organisms

Hydrogen - Makes up 9.56% of living organisms


Cell Theory

Cell theory is a scientific concept relating to the behavior of cells. It is made up of 3 basic principles. These principles can help determine if something is living or non living

  • All organisms are made from cells

  • All cells are made from other cells

  • Cells are the most basic unit of life

    • In short, anything made up of cells is living


    Characteristics Of Living Things

    All life on Earth has 8 common characteristics that are seen only in living things. These can help determine if something is living or not

  • All living things are made from cells

  • All living things grow and reproduce

  • All living things have DNA

  • All living things can heal themselves

  • All living things respire (use nutrients to make energy)

  • All living things respond to stimulii

  • All living things produce waste

  • All living things die


    • Using these 3 things, scientists can determine if something is living or not. Once something is considered living, it gets put into a group


    Classification Of Living Things

    All life originated from one common ancestor. Over billions of years, the first form of life changed and evolved into the many different forms of life we see today. Scientists needed a system to classify all these different organisms. This was done through taxonomy (grouping organisms based on similar characteristics, behaviors, or physical traits) or through phylogeny (grouping organisms based on their evolutionary past)

    Taxonomy

    Taxonomy is the system of classification based on physical traits and behaviours. Carl von Linnaeus, who created the "Linnaean" system, is considered to have been the father of modern taxonomy. His "Linnaean" classification system is something you have most likely seen before. It places different organisms into different taxa. The largest taxa is domain, while the smallest is species. Each taxon is bigger than the previous one (eg. there can be multiple species within a genus, or multiple genus within a family). Using scientific nomenclature, when naming an orgnaism, say its genus and then species

    Domains

    There are 3 different domains:

    Bacteria

  • Live in regular conditions

  • Unicellular (made up of one cell)

  • Can be heterotrophs (use other organisms for energy) or photoautotrophs (use sunlight as energy)

  • Are made from prokaryotic cells

  • Inclues the Eubacteria kingdom

    • Archaea

  • Found in extreme conditions

  • Unicellular (made up of one cell)

  • Are mostly chemoautotrophs (use chemicals as energy)

  • Are made from prokaryotic cells

  • Includes the Archaebacteria kingdom

    • Eukarya

  • Found everywhere

  • Can be single or multicelled

  • Made up of eukaryotic cells

  • Inclues the Protista, Fungi, Plantae, and Animalia kingdoms


    • Prokaryotes

    Prokaryotes are a type of cell. They are found in organisms within the Bacteria and Archaea domain. Prokaryotes have distinguishable characteristics, such as

    • No nuclear membrane

    • Circular DNA

    • No membrane bound organelles

    • Small (around 0.5 micrometers)

    • Rigid cell walls

    Eukaryotes

    Eukaryotes are a type of cell. They are found in organisms within the Eukarya. They have the typical cell structure you would be used to. Eukaryotes have distinguishable characteristics, such as

    • Has a nuclear membrane

    • DNA in form of chromosomes

    • Many membrane bound organelles

    • Large (around 40 micrometers)

    • Rigid cell walls, except in animal cells, where there is only a cell membrane

    The Eukarya Domain

    The Eukarya Domain consists of 4 kingdoms. This is where most living organisms we see daily are classified into

    Protista

  • Both single and multicelled

  • Heterotrophs (get food from other organisms) and photoautotrophs (get food through sun)

  • Aquatic (live in bodies of water)

    • Fungi

  • All are multicellular except yeasts, which are single cellular

  • Heterotrophs (get food from other organisms) by externally breaking them down

    • Plantae

  • Multicellular

  • Photoautotrophs

  • Most are terrestrial (live on land)

  • Non motile (cannot move)

    • Animalia

  • Multicellular

  • Heterotrophs

  • Are both terrestrial and aquatic

  • Motile (can move)




    • Phylogeny

    Phylogeny is the classification of living things based on DNA and past ancestors. It is represented using a phylogenetic tree. A phylogentic tree shows different evolutionary traits and evolutionary relationships between organisms. It shows derived traits between organisms. For example, this phylogenetic tree shows that all organisms after the jawless fish have a jaw. The trait is passed down to every organism. It also shows that birds and crocodiles have a fairly recent common ancestor, indicating they began to exist recently. This is the opposite of sharks, which were one of the first descendents of the common ancester shared by all organisms in this tree
